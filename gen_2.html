Cleffa revolutionized the game. it let players rely less on prof Oak. and preserve cards by shuffling cards into your deck and not discarding it, (and more recources are better than none). Ironicly, Cleffa's greatest asset was that is weakend the streanghth of hand-disrupion combos that the game was based off. Cleffa was combined with Lass, allowing players to deliver one-sided hand disruption that mimicked the hand disruption players used before. The idea was to play Lass and immediately recover a fresh hand with Eeeeeeek. Meanwhile your opponent would be left with a (usually small) Trainer-less hand. This strategy was very strong on the first turn of the game, especially if your opponent did not themself start with cleffa. Very fast to realize how powerful this combo was, players began adding 4 Cleffas to there deck. Their odds of both executing and surviveing it.

No doubt the best card from the Neo Era. in fact, the inpact on the game, it's not hard to argue that cleffa remains the best pokemon card of all time, relitiv eto itd time. But more game breacking came in the Neo era.

Murkrow, neo era


Another free retreater with no weakness, Murkrow is another solid Basic pokemon that had one of the greatest attacks anyone ever's seen. Notice how Mean Look have any "during" or "until your opponent" limitation; as long as Murkrow stayed active, so did Mean Look. This meant most Psychic-type or any pokemon not capable of dealing damage to Murkrow; who could be permanently stranded while Murkrow could start picking off pokemon on your opponents bench. The best counters to Murkrow Mean Look were Trainers that switched pokemon like "Switch" or get Murkrow off like "Gust Of Wind"

BABY POKEMON:


Pichu, Baby Pokemon, Neo Era

Pichu was a great counter to pokemon power deck's. An opponent facing Pichu would need to flip heads to knock out Pichu before it obliterated there board, but a clever strategy to keep pichu (and any baby pokemon) in play and attacking was to equip it with the new Focus Band, making the opponent need not just 1 coin flip, but two coin flip's! to score a K.O.


Magby, Baby Pokemon, Neo Era

Magby was another power-countering Baby Pokemon, but the 10 damage it did should not be overlooked, it was a great way to finish off a Pokemon a pokemon that had just survived that thanks to Focus Band.


Igglybuff, Baby Pokemon, Neo Era

although it has very scary eyes, 2-3 Igglybuff were common in deck during 2001-2002 to counter Slowking, you'll probably never need more than one in youre deck (and its a basic so it saves even more). it can be usefull aganst powers like; Darj Vileplume's Hay Fever or Aerodactyl’s Prehistoric Power.

Tyroge, Baby Pokemon, Neo Era

Tyroge offered players a chanse to K.O. baby pokemon with two lucky coin flips. Attacking with Tyroge was a strong strategy when you wanted to try to knock out your opponents Cleffa without risking giving your opponent's a flip-less Eeeeeeek.




Professor Elm, Neo Era

So, as i said, Cleffa is amazing, but it has to take the acitve pokemon slot, so would you like to have a strong pokemon at the active slot? well, Prof. Elm has you covered. An aternative to Proffesor Oak, Proffesor Elm was great whan you needed a new hand, but didn't want to discard any cards from your hand (because the name of the game is resoure management). Proffesor Elm fits into all Base-neo decks and would have likely been maxed out in them had Cleffa not debuted alongside it.
